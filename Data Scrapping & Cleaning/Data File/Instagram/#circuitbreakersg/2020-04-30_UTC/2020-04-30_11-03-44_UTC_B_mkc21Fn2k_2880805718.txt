My preferred approach is to build strength and stability in static unilateral positions before attempting movement. For the one-arm push up, adopt a normal pushup position - with legs splayed further apart for balance - and remove one hand from the floor. Do the same, but for the bottom of the pushup position. Hold these positions for time - 60s is good. You will naturally be able to do a one-arm push up when you are strong and stable in these static unilateral positions. Similarly for the pistol squat, be comfortable holding a standing and full squat position with one leg. If you can do these, the pistol is not far off. The principle is this: if you can hold the start and end positions, the movement in between should take care of itself.
