Palak Paneer Recipe:

Serves 4

500g chinese spinach (leaves only)
1 medium tomato, quartered
7 garlic cloves (4 whole and 3 finely chopped)
2 inch ginger
2 green chilli, de-seeded and roughly chopped
2 Tbsp oil
1 red onion
1/2 cup water
1 tsp garam masala powder
1/4 tsp turmeric powder 
1/4 tsp red chilli powder
Salt to taste
500g paneer cubes

1. Wash thoroughly and then blanch spinach leaves in salted, boiling water for 3-4 minutes.
2. After blanching, put spinach leaves in ice cold water.
3. Using a blender (or immersion blender), blend blanched spinach leaves, tomato, whole garlic cloves, ginger, and green chilli to a puree.
4. Heat a pan to medium heat, and add oil followed by chopped garlic. Saute for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
5. Add chopped onions to the pan. Saute for 3-4 minutes until fragrant and translucent.
6. Add in spinach puree and water. Reduce to low-medium heat and cover the pan. Let the puree cook 10 minutes, stirring frequently.
7. Add in the garam masala, turmeric powder,  and red chilli powder. Mix and cook for 1 minute. Add salt to taste.
8. Stir in the paneer cubes (refer to notes) and mix. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
9. Serve the palak paneer hot with naan.

Notes for paneer:
Before cooking, soften paneer cubes in hot water for 10 minutes.
