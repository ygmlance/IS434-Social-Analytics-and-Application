A pair of primate researchers in Singapore have called on the authorities to preserve a small forest patch along Upper Thomson Road from further development as it is currently one of the feeding grounds for the critically endangered Raffles’ banded langur.⁣
⁣
While this area is state land and is currently being used by the Singapore Armed Forces for training, it is not designated as a protected area for conservation, say the researchers.⁣
⁣
Under Urban Redevelopment Authority’s (URA) 2019 Master Plan, the area is still “subject to detailed planning” for future residential use.⁣
