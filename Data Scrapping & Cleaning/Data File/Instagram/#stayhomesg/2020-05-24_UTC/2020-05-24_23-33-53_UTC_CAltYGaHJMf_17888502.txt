Singapore's construction sector is poised to take a hard hit from the Covid-19 outbreak in the short-term, and is now expected to contract sharply by 10.3 per cent this year in real terms, according to Fitch Solutions.

Its latest view is informed by the government's decision to temporarily suspend all construction work as part of tighter circuit breaker measures to curb Covid-19 transmission, following the spike in the number of confirmed Covid-19 cases in various dormitories housing construction workers.
